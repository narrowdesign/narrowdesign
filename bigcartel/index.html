Actually, let's go back a bit further. Back to Rhode Island in the mid eighties with a kid drawing GI Joe.



GI Joe, Michaelangelo and Leonardo (the Ninja Turtles), and Michaelangelo and Leonardo (the artists) all appreciate art, but they do not appreciate having their trademarked material posted on websites. They, like a lot of folks, are stuck in the past.

Rich eventually outgrew cartoon icons and went on to study art at RISD. Where's that? (see map above)

SHOW RISD WORK?

Studied with Rick Berry. Which is pretty much a big deal. Trust us.

(Not Rick "Granny Shot" Barry. Although how cool would that be?)

Sometimes good things happen to good people like Rich. The guy has no idea how good he is. We've been trying to tell him. Watch this, "Rich! Listen to us. You rule!"

Just look at his work.

So we eventually get to June 2012 when Rich unsuspectingly opened a Big Cartel shop to sell his work, and it just happened to be our 250,000th store. It was a huge milestone for Big Cartel, so we flew Rich out to SLC, held him a one man art show, hired a band, roasted some s'mores, drank champagne, and invited a few hundred people.
