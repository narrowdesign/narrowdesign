The British Academy of Film and Television Arts Los Angeles Eighth Annual Britannia Award

On behalf of the British Academy of Film and Television Arts Los Angeles, it is my great pleasure to welcome you all to the Eighth Annual Britannia Award.

The Britannia Award is bestowed by the Academy each year in recognition of outstanding achievement in the entertainment industry. This year, Mr. John Travolta joins a truly illustrious roll of recipients, each of whom have made an unparalleled contribution deeply appreciated on both sides of the Atlantic: Dustin Hoffman, Sir Anthony Hopkins, Michael Caine, Martin Scorsese, Bob and Harvey Weinstein, Sir Peter Ustinov, and Albert R. "Cubby" Broccoli.

In the late seventies, John ignited the world-wide disco craze as Tony Manero in Saturday Night Fever. Grease, and John's lead role as Danny, launched a lasting revival of fifties music and fashion. Urban Cowboy, in which he played honky-tonk hero Bud Davis (no relation), dispersed the country and western ethos (or was it pathos) as far afield as pub jukeboxes deep in the heart of Wiltshire.

After a brief respite from leading the pop-culture, and far more welcome than the recent mini-resurgence of disco, John is now back at the top of the box-office charts as the leading man for a new era of cinema. His roles in Get Shorty and Pulp Fiction encompassed two of my favorite characters and performances of all time. The past three years of leading roles in hits such as Phenomenon, Face/Off and Primary Colors demonstrate extraordinary depth and versatility and cement Johns' place in the video aisles, from Musicals to Drama, from Action Adventure to Comedy and Romance. British film has also staged an exciting resurgence in the past decade and BAFTA LA has ridden on the crest of a wave of successful films traveling from Britain to the United States and around the world. Great commercial successes such as Four Weddings and a Funeral, Mrs. Brown, Bean, and The Full Monty are underscored by the great artistic successes of British independent films, with an increasingly more powerful presence at festivals such as Telluride, and Toronto from year to year. Supporting and sustaining this renaissance, and a long overdue British invasion of Hollywood box-offices, BAFTA LA looks forward to the ongoing involvement of all our guests here this evening and the entire Hollywood community.

J. Alan Davis
Managing Editor

The British Academy of Film and Television Arts Los Angeles (BAFTA LA ) is a professional, independent, non-profit organization, founded in 1987, whose members are drawn from all spheres of the Anglo-American film and television community. BAFTA LA is associated with BAFTA, based in the United Kingdom, which was established in London in 1947.

BAFTA LA was formed specifically to promote, foster and honor creative contribution to international film and television. BAFTA LA's individual members include actors, directors, producers, cinematographers, editors, writers, sound technicians, and production designers, as well as experts in special effects and costume design. BAFTA LA members are also involved in film finance, entertainment law, international entertainment tax planning, talent representation and marketing and distribution of filmed entertainment.

BAFTA LA offers its members a broad array of events and programs involving the brightest talents and projects in the British and American entertainment industries. Special presentations keep the membership informed of mutual concern, and the Academy works closely with the British Film Office to promote production in the U.K. BAFTA LA membership events and programs include:

· Up to three screenings per month, with filmmakers and talent Q&A's;
· The Academy Awards screening series;
· The Britannia Award;
· Evenings with: a series of "body of work" presentations with filmmakers and talent in attendance;
· First Film Foundation Screenings;
· The annual Garden Party hosted by the British Consul-General;
· The BAFTA/Oscar nominees Tea Party;
· The UCLA Student Fellowship, awarded annually.

Together with the members of BAFTA in the U.K., BAFTA LA's individual members vote in the determination of the British Academy Awards. BAFTA La's company members and sponsors are dedicated to and support the goals and spirit of the Academy and enjoy a close affiliation with and involvement in the Academy's various activities.

Chief among BAFTA LA's activities is the Britannia Award, an annual formal tribute held in honor of a distinguished member of the entertainment industry. The 1998 recipient is John Travolta. The previous honorees of the Britannia Award are Dustin Hoffman, Bob and Harvey Weinstein, Sir Anthony Hopkins, Sir Peter Ustinov, Michael Caine, Martin Scorsese and Albert R. "Cubby" Broccoli.

A Banner Year

Since last year's Britannia Award presentation to Dustin Hoffman, members of BAFTA LA have enjoyed a host of activities bot social and professional.

The British Academy of Film and Television Arts Los Angeles has organized screenings of twenty-six feature films, providing members with a new film every two weeks. WE have now graduated from the Pacific Design Center and this year we have arranged ten screenings at the Writers Guild Theater, nine at The Peltz Theater, Museum of Tolerance and have had individual screenings on the lots at FOX, Warner Bros. and Paramount.

In addition to our very popular screenings, in April BAFTA LA was proud to present New Directions: A Program of Six New British Short Films which, as last year, proved a resounding success and an opportunity for young British directors and producers to meet resident producers, agents and managers.

Just over a month ago we welcomed one of our most prestigious actors with An Evening with Sir Ian McKellen, a splendid evening which inaugurated our series of intimate soirees with eminent members of our profession.

Other highlights of the preceding year include our Twelfth Annual Garden Party at the British Consul-General's residence in July, during which the BAFTA LA Scholarships were presented to Joby Harold, recipient of the BAFTA LA Fellowship, and Donte Dollar-Wright, recipient of the Diana, Princess of Wales/BAFTA LA Fellowship, both studyingat the UCLA School of Theater, Film and Television. Our fifth annual Nominees Tea Party, held in March, honored the nominees for both the British and American Academy Awards.

All in all, another highly successful year for BAFTA LA culminating in tonight's tribute to our 1998 honoree, the perennially popular John Travolta.

John Travolta

Has been honored twice with Academy Award nominations, most recently for his riveting portrayal of a philosophical hit man in Quentin Tarantino's Pulp Fiction. He has also received a Golden Globe nomination for this highly acclaimed role and was named Best Actor by the Los Angeles Film Critics Association, among other distinguished awards. He was equally praised as a Mafioso-turned-movie-producer in the comedy sensation Get Shorty, singled out by many critics as one of the best performances of the year and garnering a Golden Globe Award fro Best Actor in a Motion Picture, Musical or Coedy.

He previously starred in some of the most momentous films of our generation. He earned his first Oscar and Golden Globe nominations for his role in the blockbuster Saturday Night Fever, which launched the disco phenomenon of the late 1970's. He went on to star in the mega-hit screen version of the long-running musical Grease and the wildly successful Urban Cowboy. Additional film credits include the Brian De Palma thriller Carrie and Blow Out, as well as Amy Heckerling's hit comedy, Look Who's Talking. Travolta has starred in Michael and Phenomenon and took an equally diverse turn as an action star in John Woo's Broken Arrow. He has also starred in Face/Off with Nicholas Cage and in She's So Lovely with Sean Penn and Robin Wright-Penn, as well as in Mad City, co-starring Dustin Hoffman. He recently starred in Primary Colors opposite Emma Thompson for director Mike Nichols and has completed filming on A Civil Action with director Steve Zaillian.
