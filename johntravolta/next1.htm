On the Road . . .
This month, John Travolta kicks off a multi-city domestic press tour for Warner Bros.' big-budget sci-fi thriller Battlefield Earth, based on the bestseller by Scientology-founder L. Ron Hubbard. In May, the actor, who's both star and producer of the movie, will head for the Continent to begin a European tour leading up to the movie's May 12 premier.

In the Studio . . .
In Battlefield Earth, Travolta will play Terl, the ruthless security chief of the Psychlos, giant, gas-breathing alien invaders who have dominated Earth for 10 centuries. Now, in the year 3000, Johnnie Goodboy Tyler (Barry Pepper), one of only a handful of human beings who have survived the Psychlos' near-annihilation of the planet, has come down from the Rocky Mountains to confront his conquerors. Initially captured and imprisoned by Terl, he escapes to lead a band of survivors determined to rid their planet of Terl and his henchmen. To play Terl, Travolta had to wear an eight-foot-tall costume rig, which included stilts, long claws and a giant head.


On the Lot . . .
Paramount Studios is in the final stages of preparing for the July release of Travolta's second movie for 2000, director Nora Ephron's Numbers, in which the actor plays TV weatherman Russ Richards, whose lavish lifestyle and bad business investments, including a gone-bust snowmobile dealership, have brought him to the brink of bankruptcy. To extricate himself, Richards cooks up a plot with the station's lotto ball girl (Lisa Kudrow), to rig the state lottery drawing. Immediately, though, the balls start bouncing the wrong way.
