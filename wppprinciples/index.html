Principles

These principles were developed during quarantine in the fall of 2020—when Stripe was a ~3000 person company—by the 10 members of the Web Presence and Platform team. At the time of their initial creation, we were a manager, 2 visual designers, and 7 engineers. The following are not rules. They may be useful guides. Some are intentionally counterintuitive. We reasoned that if they were obvious, if they could be arrived at instinctively, they wouldn’t be memorable and we wouldn’t have needed to write them down. What we hope is that they’ll enrich our language, make our window of time together at Stripe more enjoyable, and contribute to improving everything we make together in that time. They should change with us in quantity and quality. They should be discarded as they lose their usefulness.

by @amurray
01

We are responsible for thoughtfully bringing the Stripe brand to life on the web. The systems we operate in service of this brand are complex and continuously evolving. Before improvements can be made confidently, attention must be paid to the context both within and surrounding these systems. Urgency without focus will lead us in the wrong direction. In order to deeply understand existing conditions and how we can improve them, we can optimize for time to observe and time to think. If we resist the rush to make change before we are clear-eyed about who our efforts will impact, we’ll improve the odds of an outcome they’ll appreciate and in the process, create systems our future selves will thank us for.

02

Relationships first. Always. Caring about every detail of our work and being a pleasure to work with are not mutually exclusive. If we are as committed to treating each other with care as we are to making every web experience special, our team culture will be as admired as our work. We won’t always work together. Some project will be this group’s last. By recognizing this, we can seize every opportunity to support each other, elevate each others work, and create opportunities for each other. The amount we enjoy our window of time together at Stripe and the measure of our collective impact will depend in large part on how consistently we show up for each other.

Collaboration

We are surrounded by teammates full of agency. Vulnerability in this environment can be such a challenge that problem solving in isolation might seem like the preferred path. Before taking action alone, leverage the expertise of collaborators. Enlisting help early and often is a mutually beneficial step. It provides opportunities to connect others with our work. There is world-class talent on this team and we’re eager to put it to use! The right contribution at the right moment can mean the difference between meeting the requirements of a project and elevating both the ambitions of a teammate and the brand.

04

While every project lists a different set of contributors, the only name we send into the world together is “Stripe.” Creating Stripe’s websites means we are directly shaping the Stripe brand. Everything we make has some effect on the strength of that brand and on every person, internally and externally, who relies on it. By paying constant attention to the system we are operating within and being thoughtful about the impact of each step, the imprint this team leaves on Stripe will be more than a list of names.

05
Contribution
Stripe OP category

The right step is the one leading toward future systems we’d want to inherit. Within those future systems our work builds trust among users, attracts world-class talent, and drives the web forward. We can’t know exactly what a future teammate will need but we know they won’t want to clean up our mess. We should prioritize difficult steps our future selves and teammates will thank us for taking. By taking time to understand the present context, agreeing on the future we hope to create, and facing the forces working against us, we can relieve the pressure of pretending to be prophets. The things we code, design, and write today, not what we intend to do later, will determine our impact on the brand, those who come after us, and the web.

Prioritize trust over caution
06

When a teammate makes the not-so-obvious, obvious, we learn. When they display dedication to improving the experience of our customers, we are inspired. But it‘s through getting uncomfortable, through struggling out in the open, that we thrive. If we fear failure, it can scare us away from our most ambitious ideas. If we give failure a seat at the table, it can illuminate opportunities to innovate, uncover new possibilities, and strengthen the bond we build as a team. The strength of that bond will determine whether we just get by, or thrive together. By redefining vulnerability, we can ensure that bond remains ironclad.

by @tatiana
07

Mountains are climbed one step at a time and the terrain of each slope determines the direction of the best next step. It’s not always up. The same is true of every project. We consider the quality of each step in the creation process and the potential impact it will have on the the user, the team, and the brand. To ensure each step lands successfully, we take the time to zoom in and zoom out, to try things and to abandon them in favor of a better path until we are confident in which next step is best.

08

Endless theorizing about solutions and the pitfalls of those solutions paralyzes progress. A thing that exists, even in a blurry form, can be improved more meaningfully than the idea of a thing. Once it can be observed in its context, a solution can be evaluated and shaped to fit. If it leads in a direction we don’t wish to go, we can turn back without fear of abandoning a precious theory. When assumptions are cleared away we can turn our attention to identifying opportunities to deliver unexpected improvements.

by @nick

What is expected is forgotten. Whether it’s something removed or something extra, what stands out is what is remembered. Principles are unnecessary. Team traditions are unnecessary. Konami codes are unnecessary. We do these things for enjoyment and for each other. It’s fine if nobody else notices, but we know from experience that people do notice the unnecessary things. The unnecessary things inspire people to join our team and use our product. They are evidence to anyone considering Stripe that behind our products is a human being who puts an uncommon level of effort into the things they make. Only by going to lengths nobody else would can we elevate Stripe on the web and in the world.

Elevate our brand
Prioritize progress over predictability

Results
Stripe OP category

For many, Stripe’s website is our brand. Stripe users and industry onlookers don’t all come to our website for information, they come for inspiration because they trust the Stripe brand and aspire to what it represents. This high esteem places high expectations on everything we produce. To remain “venturesome” as Patrick put it, requires continuously exceeding those expectations. The futures of our customers, fellow Stripes, and each individual on this team depend on the Stripe brand remaining strong. To elevate it means delivering systems and experiences of a quality no reasonable person would expect from us.
